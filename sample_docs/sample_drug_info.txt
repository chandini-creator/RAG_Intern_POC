Aspirin (acetylsalicylic acid)
- Use: Pain relief, fever reduction, anti-inflammatory. Low dose used for cardiovascular protection.
- Dosing: Adults 325–650 mg every 4–6 hours as needed; max 4 g/day. Low dose for cardio: 75–100 mg once daily.
- Warnings: Avoid in bleeding disorders, active ulcers. Reye's syndrome risk in children with viral illness.

Ibuprofen
- Use: Pain, fever, inflammation (e.g. arthritis, headache).
- Dosing: Adults 200–400 mg every 4–6 hours; max 1200 mg/day OTC, up to 3200 mg/day Rx.
- Warnings: GI bleeding risk, kidney injury with long-term use. Avoid in late pregnancy.

Acetaminophen (paracetamol)
- Use: Pain and fever. Preferred when NSAIDs are contraindicated.
- Dosing: Adults 325–650 mg every 4–6 hours or 1000 mg every 6 hours; max 3000–4000 mg/day.
- Warnings: Hepatotoxicity at high doses or with alcohol. Check combination products to avoid overdose.
